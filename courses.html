Most Recommended Book

Atomic Habit

"Atomic Habits: An Easy & Proven Way to Build Good Habits & Break Bad Ones" is a self-help book by James Clear that delves into the science of habit formation and offers practical strategies for creating positive change in one's life. The book is grounded in research from fields like psychology, neuroscience, and behavioral economics, and it presents a comprehensive guide to understanding and improving habits

Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck

"The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck: A Counterintuitive Approach to Living a Good Life" is a self-help book by Mark Manson that challenges conventional wisdom about what constitutes a happy and successful life. The book promotes the idea that not everything in life is worth caring about, and that focusing on what truly matters can lead to a more fulfilling existence.

Think and Grow Rich

"Think and Grow Rich" is a classic self-help book written by Napoleon Hill, first published in 1937. It is one of the best-selling books of all time, offering timeless advice on how to achieve personal and financial success. The book distills Hill’s philosophy of success, which he developed after studying the habits and practices of numerous successful individuals over many years.

Alchemist

"The Alchemist" is a philosophical novel written by Brazilian author Paulo Coelho. First published in 1988, it has since become a worldwide bestseller and has been translated into numerous languages. The book tells the story of Santiago, a young Andalusian shepherd, and his journey to discover his personal legend and fulfill his dreams.

Poor Dad Rich Dad

"Rich Dad Poor Dad: What the Rich Teach Their Kids About Money That the Poor and Middle Class Do Not!" is a personal finance book written by Robert T. Kiyosaki. First published in 1997, the book advocates for financial literacy and independence through investing, real estate, owning businesses, and enhancing financial intelligence.

Psychology of Money

"The Psychology of Money: Timeless Lessons on Wealth, Greed, and Happiness" is a book written by Morgan Housel, first published in 2020. It explores the emotional and psychological aspects of money and how they influence our financial decisions. The book is a collection of 20 short stories that illustrate the complex relationship people have with money, offering insights and practical wisdom on how to manage finances more effectively.

Who Moved My Cheese?

"Who Moved My Cheese?: An A-Mazing Way to Deal with Change in Your Work and in Your Life" is a motivational business fable written by Dr. Spencer Johnson. First published in 1998, the book uses a simple story to illustrate profound truths about dealing with change in work and life. It has sold millions of copies worldwide and has been widely used in management and motivational training.

Ikigai

"Ikigai: The Japanese Secret to a Long and Happy Life" is a book written by Héctor García and Francesc Miralles. Published in 2016, the book explores the Japanese concept of "ikigai," which translates to "a reason for being" or "a reason to wake up in the morning." The authors combine personal anecdotes, research, and insights from interviews with the residents of Okinawa, a region in Japan known for its high number of centenarians, to illustrate how ikigai can contribute to a fulfilling and meaningful life.

How to Win Friends & Influence People

"How to Win Friends and Influence People" is a classic self-help book written by Dale Carnegie, first published in 1936. The book provides practical advice and techniques on how to improve interpersonal skills, build better relationships, and influence others positively. It has been a perennial bestseller and remains highly relevant for anyone looking to enhance their social and communication skills.

Sapiens

"Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind" is a non-fiction book written by Yuval Noah Harari, first published in Hebrew in 2011 and in English in 2014. The book provides a comprehensive and thought-provoking exploration of the history of Homo sapiens, tracing our evolution from ancient hunter-gatherer societies to the modern complexities of civilization.

Master your Emotion

"Master Your Emotions: A Practical Guide to Overcome Negativity and Better Manage Your Feelings" is a book by Thibaut Meurisse, published in 2018. It offers insights and strategies to help individuals understand and effectively manage their emotions in order to lead a more fulfilling and balanced life.

To Kill a Mockingbird

"To Kill a Mockingbird" is a novel written by Harper Lee, first published in 1960. Set in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama, during the 1930s, the book explores themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and compassion through the eyes of its young protagonist, Scout Finch.
