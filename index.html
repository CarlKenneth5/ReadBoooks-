Benefits of Reading Books

Calling book lovers and avid readers of all ages! Have you ever wondered what the benefits of reading are aside from leisure and education? From learning new words to maintaining your mental health, books can do it all! In case you needed a reminder of how important regular reading is for our wellbeing and literacy, here are some benefits of reading books

Learn More



Reading Exercises the Brain

While reading, we have to remember different characters and settings that belong to a given story. Even if you enjoy reading a book in one sitting, you have to remember the details throughout the time you take to read the book. Therefore, reading is a workout for your brain that improves memory function.

Reading is a Form of (free) Entertainment

Did you know that most of the popular TV shows and movies are based on books? So why not indulge in the original form of entertainment by immersing yourself in reading. Most importantly, it’s free with your Markham Public Library card.

Reading Improves Concentration and the Ability to Focus

We can all agree that reading cannot happen without focus and in order to fully understand the story, we have to concentrate on each page that we read. In a world where gadgets are only getting faster and shortening our attention span, we need to constantly practice concentration and focus. Reading is one of the few activities that requires your undivided attention, therefore, improving your ability to concentrate.

Reading Improves Sleep

By creating a bedtime routine that includes reading, you can signal to your body that it is time to sleep. Now, more than ever, we rely on increased screen time to get through the day. Therefore, by setting your phone aside and picking up a book, you are telling your brain that it is time to quiet down. Moreover, since reading helps you de-stress, doing so right before bed helps calm your mind and anxiety and improve the quality of sleep.

Reading Increases General Knowledge

Books are always filled with fun and interesting facts. Whether you read fiction or non-fictions, books have the ability to provide us with information we would’ve otherwise not known. Reading a variety of topics can make you a more knowledgeable person, in turn improving your conversation skills.

Reading Reduces Stress

Reading has the power to transport you to another world and away from the monotonous daily routine. By doing so, reading can decrease stress, lower heart rate and reduce blood pressure.

Most Popular Types of Books

Self-Help Book

A self-help book is a type of nonfiction book designed to guide readers in solving personal problems and improving various aspects of their lives. These books often offer advice, strategies, and practical steps on a wide range of topics, including mental health, relationships, personal finance, productivity, motivation, and personal growth.

Learn More

Romance Novel

A romance novel is a genre of fiction that focuses on the romantic relationship between two (or sometimes more) characters. The primary plot revolves around the romantic interactions, conflicts, and eventual resolution of the relationship, usually culminating in a happy or emotionally satisfying ending.

Learn More

Mystery

A mystery is a genre of fiction that revolves around the solving of a crime, puzzle, or unexplained event. The central plot typically involves a protagonist—often a detective or an amateur sleuth—who works to uncover the truth behind the mystery through investigation, deduction, and the piecing together of clues.

Learn More

Fantasy

Fantasy is a genre of fiction that involves magical or supernatural elements that do not exist in the real world. These elements can include magic, mythical creatures, fantastical worlds, and extraordinary adventures. Fantasy often explores themes of good versus evil, heroism, and the quest for power or self-discovery.


Learn More

Horror

Horror is a genre of fiction intended to provoke fear, dread, and shock in its audience. It often delves into the darkest aspects of human nature and explores themes of the supernatural, the unknown, and the macabre. Horror stories are designed to evoke an emotional response, typically involving suspense, terror, and a sense of the uncanny or grotesque.

Learn More

Classic

The term "classic" in literature refers to works that have stood the test of time and are widely recognized for their outstanding quality, enduring significance, and universal appeal. These works often offer profound insights into human nature, society, and the human condition. Classic literature is known for its high artistic merit, complex characters, and well-crafted narratives.

Frequent Asked Question

1. Why is reading books important?

Reading books is important for several reasons

Knowledge and Learning : Books provide information, insights, and perspectives on various subjects, helping readers expand their knowledge.

Improving Language Skills : Reading enhances vocabulary, grammar, and comprehension skills.

Critical Thinking : Books challenge readers to think critically, analyze information, and form their own opinions.

Empathy and Understanding : Fiction and non-fiction books alike can foster empathy by exposing readers to diverse experiences and perspectives

2. How can I develop a reading habit?

Developing a reading habit takes consistency and effort:

Set Goals : Start with achievable reading goals, such as a certain number of pages or chapters per day.

Create a Reading Routine : Establish a specific time and place for reading, whether it's in the morning, during lunch breaks, or before bedtime.

Choose Books You Enjoy : Pick books that interest you or align with your hobbies and passions.

Eliminate Distractions : Minimize distractions like phones or TV when reading.

Join a Book Club : Participating in a book club can provide accountability and opportunities for discussion.

3. How do I choose what books to read?

Choosing books to read depends on personal preferences and goals

Genres and Interests : Consider what genres or topics you enjoy. Experiment with new genres to broaden your reading experience.

Recommendations : Seek recommendations from friends, family, book reviews, or online forums.

Bestseller Lists : Explore current bestseller lists or award-winning books.

Book Reviews : Read reviews to gauge whether a book aligns with your interests and reading preferences

4. What are the benefits of reading fiction vs. non-fiction?

Both fiction and non-fiction offer unique benefits:

Fiction : Enhances empathy by immersing readers in diverse characters' experiences. Stimulates imagination and creativity. Offers escapism and entertainment.

Non-Fiction : Provides factual information and insights into real-world topics. Enhances knowledge and understanding of specific subjects. Can inspire personal growth and self-improvement.

5. How can I read more effectively and retain information?

Reading effectively involves active engagement and comprehension:

Preview and Skim : Before diving into a book, skim the table of contents, chapter headings, and summaries to get an overview.

Take Notes : Jot down key points, quotes, and thoughts while reading to aid retention.

Ask Questions : Engage with the material by asking yourself questions about the content or characters.

Reflect and Review : Take time to reflect on what you've read and how it relates to your own experiences or knowledge

6. What should I do if I don't enjoy a book I'm reading?

Not every book will resonate with every reader:

Give It a Chance : Sometimes, books take time to captivate. Consider giving it a few more chapters before deciding.

Set It Aside : It's okay to set aside a book if it doesn't capture your interest. You can always revisit it later.

Find Alternatives : Explore other books in different genres or by different authors that might be more appealing to you.

7. How can I incorporate more reading into a busy schedule?

Even with a busy schedule, you can find time for reading:

Schedule Reading Time : Allocate specific times during the day for reading, such as during breaks, commuting, or before bed.

Multitask : Listen to audiobooks while exercising, cooking, or doing household chores.

Set Realistic Goals : Start with small reading goals, such as reading for 10-15 minutes a day, and gradually increase as you build the habit.

8. How can I improve my reading speed and comprehension?

Improving reading speed and comprehension takes practice:

Practice Regularly : Read consistently to improve both speed and comprehension.

Expand Vocabulary : Build your vocabulary to enhance reading fluency and understanding.

Use Reading Aids: Use tools like bookmarks, highlighters, or apps that promote speed reading techniques.

Summarize and Discuss : Summarize chapters or sections after reading and discuss them with others to reinforce understanding.

9. What are some tips for reading more critically?

Critical reading involves analyzing and evaluating the content:

Ask Questions : Question the author's arguments, evidence, and conclusions.

Compare Perspectives : Consider different viewpoints and interpretations of the text.

Evaluate Sources : Assess the credibility and reliability of the information presented.

Contextualize : Understand the historical, cultural, and social context in which the book was written

10. How can I make reading a social activity?

Reading can be a social activity that fosters discussion and connection:

Join a Book Club : Participate in a local or online book club to discuss books with others.

Share Recommendations : Exchange book recommendations with friends, family, or colleagues.

Attend Literary Events : Attend author readings, book signings, or literary festivals to engage with fellow book lovers.

Use Social Media : Follow book-related hashtags or communities on social media platforms to share thoughts and insights on books.
